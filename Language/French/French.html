French

It is a language spoken around the world due to colonization and in much of continental France due to restrictive language planning (France had and otherwise would have a very rich linguistic heritage - an Occitan writer was awarded the 1904 Nobel prize in literature, in fact! But noooooooooooo God forbid diglossia exist in a country; we have to shame children for speaking their mother tongue!)

Besides France, some other places where it is unrightfully spoken include:

  • Québec
  • Acadia
  • Louisiana (but here French was being oppressed in the 20th century, rather than being the one used for oppressing, so it feels a bit mean to rage against it.)
  • Haiti
  • Much of West and Central Africa (Cameroon, Côte d'Ivoire, DRC, Senegal...)
  • Lebanon, Syria, Morocco, Algeria, Tunisia

I am interested in learning a Québécois variety, both because I think it sounds cooler (love the diphthongs) and because I want to piss off Europeans.
